Professional Experience

Research Intern – IISc Bengaluru

During my research internship at the Indian Institute of Science (IISc), I independently conceptualized and developed a predictive model to personalize Rh immunoglobulin (RhIg) dosage in Rh incompatibility cases. The project addresses the limitations of standard fixed-dose administration by tailoring dosage based on patient-specific parameters, enhancing both safety and clinical efficacy. I handled the entire pipeline — from biomedical research and data analysis to model design, implementation, and validation. This precision-based approach has the potential to significantly improve maternal-fetal outcomes. Owing to its novelty and clinical relevance, the work is currently being evaluated for patent filing.

Neuromorphic Computing – CFM, VIT

As part of the Centre for Functional Materials (CFM) at VIT, I contributed to the simulation of neuromorphic computing architectures using BaTiO₃ substrates. My work involved exploring the properties of ferroelectric materials and their influence on charge-based logic for brain-inspired computing. I was responsible for material analysis using lab-generated data and helped refine models that mimic biological synaptic behavior. This internship allowed me to gain hands-on experience in nanoelectronics and experimental materials science.

Development Intern – Innoviti Technologies

During my internship at Innoviti Technologies, I identified and resolved a critical ASO (Assistant Section Officer) mapping error that was affecting payment device distribution. The issue caused incorrect mappings in specific scenarios, leading to inconsistencies. I proposed and implemented a corrected mapping logic that improved consistency by over 90%. I also collaborated with QA and DevOps teams to ensure thorough testing and a smooth rollout of the fix. This experience strengthened my problem-solving skills and gave me hands-on exposure to debugging in live systems. The fix contributed to more reliable backend operations within the organization.
